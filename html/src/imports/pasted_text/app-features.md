## Money Mentor — Complete Feature Descriptions

---

### 1. AI Chat Advisor

The heart of the app. Instead of paying a financial advisor thousands of rupees per session, you simply type your question in plain English and get a personalized, data-driven answer instantly.

The AI doesn't give you generic textbook advice. It knows who you are — your age, income, savings, risk tolerance, and goals — and answers accordingly. Ask it how Reliance Industries is performing and it pulls live stock data: current price, percentage change, P/E ratio, trend direction, and 52-week high/low range, then tells you whether to hold, buy, or reconsider based on your portfolio. Ask it to compare TCS and Infosys and it gives you a side-by-side breakdown of price, valuation, growth outlook, and a specific recommendation tailored to what you already own. Ask it "based on my profile, what should be my next steps?" and it reads your actual financial data — your savings rate, investment level, goals — and gives you a structured action plan with specific rupee amounts and allocation percentages.

This is not a chatbot that gives Wikipedia answers. It is a financial advisor that knows your money and advises accordingly, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, with no commission bias and no appointment needed.

---

### 2. Your Financial Profile

The foundation that powers everything else in the app. Before any advice is given, Money Mentor gets to know you through a one-time profile setup. You fill in your name, age, monthly income, monthly expenses, current savings, total investments, current debt, emergency fund coverage in months, whether you have insurance, and your financial goals.

You also set your risk profile — Low, Medium, or High — which tells the entire app how aggressively or conservatively to advise you. Someone with a high risk profile gets different stock recommendations than someone with a low risk profile, even with identical income and savings.

The power of the profile is that it flows through every single feature. When the AI gives advice, it references your profile. When the health score calculates your grade, it uses your savings rate and investment numbers. When the FIRE calculator runs, it uses your monthly expenses to compute your retirement number. When the portfolio tracker makes suggestions, it checks whether they align with your stated goals. You set it up once and the entire app becomes personalized to you.

---

### 3. Money Health Score

Think of this as your financial credit score, but one that actually tells you something useful and actionable. The health score rates your overall financial wellness out of 100 by evaluating you across four specific categories.

The first is Emergency Fund, worth 25 points. If you have 3 to 6 months of expenses saved and set aside as an emergency buffer, you score full marks. The second is Savings Rate, also worth 25 points. If you are saving around 40% of your income, you get an excellent rating. If your savings rate is lower, your score drops and the app tells you by how much and what to do about it. The third is Investments, worth 25 points. This is where most young earners lose marks — having only ₹10,000 invested on a ₹1 lakh income scores just 6 out of 25. The app flags this clearly and tells you to start or increase SIP contributions. The fourth is Debt and Insurance, worth 25 points. Zero debt combined with active insurance coverage earns full marks here.

Your total score maps to a letter grade from A to F with a one-line summary of your financial health and a targeted tip addressing your single weakest area. Hit "Refresh Score" at any time and it recalculates using your latest data.

---

### 4. Investment Portfolio Tracker

This is the feature that separates Money Mentor from basic finance apps. It does not just show you what you own — it actively analyzes your portfolio, projects where it is headed, measures it against your goals, and tells you exactly what adjustments to make.

You add all your investments across every asset class: mutual funds (both SIP and lump sum), stocks listed on NSE and BSE, fixed deposits, gold and Sovereign Gold Bonds, PPF, NPS, and any other instrument. The dashboard then shows your total portfolio value, the allocation breakdown across asset classes, and the individual performance of each holding.

The future growth projection is where it becomes powerful. For each investment, the tracker applies realistic return assumptions per asset class — roughly 12% for equity mutual funds, 7% for fixed deposits, 10% for gold — and projects your portfolio value at the 1 year, 3 year, 5 year, and 10 year marks. A visual growth curve shows how your wealth compounds over time so you can see it, not just read about it.

The goal-based intelligence layer connects your investments to your actual financial goals. If your goal is to buy a house in 7 years for ₹80 lakh, the tracker calculates where your current portfolio will land in 7 years, identifies the gap, and gives you a specific action plan to close it — for example, increasing your SIP by ₹4,200 per month or shifting a portion of your FD allocation into equity funds for better returns.

The AI advisor plugs directly into your portfolio data and proactively surfaces insights like over-concentration in a single sector, a mismatch between your stated medium risk profile and your actual aggressive allocation, or an FD earning 6.5% while inflation is running at 5.5%, leaving you with barely 1% in real returns. It also sends rebalancing alerts when one asset class drifts too far from your target allocation.

---

### 5. FIRE Calculator

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. This calculator answers the question every ambitious young earner eventually asks: how long until I never have to work again?

You input five numbers: your monthly SIP amount, your expected annual return percentage, your target corpus, your current investments, and your monthly expenses. The calculator instantly outputs four results. First, it tells you how many years until you hit your target — for example, 15 years. Second, it gives you your FIRE number, which is the total corpus you need to sustain your lifestyle entirely from investment returns without working — calculated as roughly 25 times your annual expenses. Third, it shows you the total amount you will have personally invested over that period. Fourth, it shows you the wealth you gained purely from compounding returns — often more than double what you actually put in.

A growth curve chart visualizes your wealth building year by year, showing the line where your invested amount ends and where your return-driven growth begins. This is the visual moment where people truly understand compounding — when they see that of their ₹1 crore final corpus, only ₹36 lakh came from their own pocket and ₹64 lakh came from the market doing the work.

---

### 6. What-If Simulator

The most engaging and interactive tool in the app. The What-If Simulator lets you test financial decisions before committing to them, turning abstract numbers into concrete, visual outcomes in seconds.

You select a scenario — such as SIP Growth Projection — and adjust the variables: monthly SIP amount, investment duration in years, and expected annual return. Hit Simulate and the app instantly calculates your total invested amount, your final portfolio value, and your net wealth gained from returns. A bar chart then shows the year-by-year growth side by side — the gray bars representing how much you actually put in each year versus the teal bars representing the total value including returns.

The practical value is enormous. A 21-year-old wondering "what happens if I just invest ₹5,000 a month?" can see in three seconds that over 10 years at 12% returns, their ₹6 lakh of contributions grow to ₹11.62 lakh — nearly doubling their money. Change the SIP to ₹10,000 and see the outcome instantly. Change the duration to 20 years and watch compounding turn that into something life-changing. The simulator makes financial planning feel less like a spreadsheet and more like a game where every variable you pull produces an immediate, tangible result.

---

### Supporting Features

Beyond the six core features, Money Mentor also includes a full dark mode toggle for comfortable use in any lighting condition, a clean mobile-first sidebar navigation system with Chat, Health Score, FIRE Calculator, and What-If Simulator accessible in one tap, a Clear Chat option to start fresh conversations, and a Logout feature. The entire interface is designed to feel like a modern fintech app — not a government portal or a traditional banking interface — making financial planning feel approachable for a generation that grew up on clean, intuitive digital products.
